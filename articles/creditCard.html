Introduction

The topic has been intensely discussed over the years, because of the high demand in the banking industry. There are a lot of solutions that have incredible results. Over the years solutions using deep learning methods, artificial neural network, random forests or logistic regression were implemented, but we will focus only focus on the comparison between the algorithms that implement the idea of binary classification. The most important aspect would be the comparison between the most respected algorithms like decision tree (of course boosted), logistic regression, and artificial neural network. The dataset will be taken from real life cases in order to be more precise. The humanity goes through hard times because of the global pandemic. The lockdown broke the spirit of so many people and whole businesses moved online, including shops. This is a good thing but also a bad thing since the most common method of payment is a credit card. A Review

Credit card fraud detection is a discussed and important topic which has received a lot of attention from the academic world. And this is not a new thing. For example, in 1997 Ginel, Cruz and Dorronsoro had created a fraud detection system that was built online and was based on a neural classifier. Also, McDonald and Bell in 1996 have created a system that was based on logit techniques but since then the problem continued to exist and become even more severe. So not only the academic world was involved but also the governments. And in the paper [2] there is a more detailed list based on the article, method and also the country. The most used techniques were based on binary classification using neural networks, Bayesian methods, decision trees

Binary Classification Methods

As we have seen from Fig2 the most used methods for solving this problem are binary classification methods so we will focus on that. But also after reading the findings of papers [1][4] I chose to focus on the comparison and of three methods: artificial neural network, decision trees, and logistic regression. 3.1 Data Set A crucial part for the success of a machine learning algorithm is the data itself. This time the article [1] suggests the use of data that comes from real-time transactions and the history database with more than 40 fields. This should be easily applied to any bank that is why this schema was chosen. From all records about 0.07% are frauds so this means the set is imbalanced so it would clearly need a pre-processing. So the values that were missing were ignored, log transformation was applied, and the key variables were extracted leaving with the Fig3 table of variables. This problem had many solutions, but we will focus on the artificial neural network, logistic regression and decision tree. So, we will start with neural networks that are formed by grouping nodes into layers and connecting these layers with some interconnections that have different weighted values. The problem with this as the paper [5] proves it: " illogical weightings of the indicators and overfitting in the training stage both of which negatively impacts predictive accuracy.". However, it was still used because it is considered better in the pure theoretical aspect than a classical statistical method in the fraud detection application. Decision tree is a classic and easy to understand method that have the power to deal with constant data. The main perfect decision tree would be a tree with high precision and small scale. The reason it would be a perfect match for the credit card problem is that is very flexible because of its non-parameter method and also it is explainable. Logistic Regression comes in handy when dealing with data where we want to predict if a certain characteristic is present or not based on a set of predictor variables.

Experimental Results

The data for training and tested was composed of transactions from 2006 and data from 2005 just for testing. The metric used to measure the performance was lift since it is commonly used on classification application. In lift metric the data its's sorted in descended order by the posterior probabilities and then dividing records into deciles. The transactions are placed into a category based on their response. In the left column there are out deciles with 0.1 corresponding to the highest responder. The average response rate is 0.07% . Fig4. The results from paper in lift [1] In fig4 we can see the actual results from the experiment. We notice that in the highest lift is located in the top decile and it is given by an artificial neural network 5.88, followed by the logit regression 5.84 and decision trees with 3.89. and with the lift chart from fig5 we can be sure that the neural network is the best one in lift metric followed by logit regression and in the end decision trees. Fig5. Lift chart [1] However, in the paper [6] it is shown that decision trees are mentioned in 19 articles even though their performance in lift metric is not so good, while the logit regression it's mentioned 8 times. The king still is an artificial neural network with 20 articles describing this solution. In the same study it is proven that both artificial neural network and decision trees have medium accuracy medium coverage but high cost in contrast with logistic regression that possess 100% true positive accuracy and medium coverage and cost. The lift metric was used because we wanted to test the performance of the model itself. Also, since the data was imbalanced even after pre-processing then this was the correct result. The formula for lift calculus is quite simple: lift = (predicted rate / average rate)

Conclusion

Credit card fraud is a real problem that can cause so many problems and it should be stopped at any costs. As we've seen the number of articles trying to deliver a solution is incredible showing the implication of researchers. The most used method is an artificial neural network which shows incredible results in lift metrics. But logistic regression and decision trees both have its advantages. In the following years we may see a rise in credit card frauds since everything is moving online and this pandemic only accelerated the process. Beside governments a lot of banks are still investing in the research of this topic in order to keep regular people's money safe. In addition, companies like Microsoft stepped in with different solution. Their solution is based on a super boosted decision tree called Fast Tree. This involvement can take us one step closer to finding and implementing a universal solution that could reduce to 0 the credit card frauds. Further work can be done on this topic by analyzing more results from different articles. And note what is the difference in the methods. This can lead to more discoveries that can have a positive impact on the subject.
